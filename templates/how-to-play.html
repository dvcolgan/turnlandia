Overview

Welcome to Turn Based Game, a massively multiplayer online board game (MMOBG).

The goal of this game is to control land and defend it from other players by strategically allocating your units.

Setup

To play you must first create an account. We will ask for the following information:

  • Username and password - used to log in
  • Email - optional, if you set it, we will email you an activity summary every day (coming soon!)
  • A team color - all of your units will use this color, as well as tiles you own
  • A leader name - be creative and make up a title for yourself
  • A people name - similarly, make up a name for the people in your empire

Choosing an initial settlement

After this you will be taken to the game board. In order to begin playing, you need to set up a home base. Your initial encampment must not contain any other player's territory.

From here on, you can click on a button at the top of the screen and then click on a square to apply that action to that square:

  • Place Unit - take a unit from your unused unit pile and place it on that square. You can place a unit on any square you control or any square that is adjacent to a square you control. The number next to this button shows how many you have left to place.
  • Remove Unit - remove a unit from that square and add it to your unused pile.
  • Settle Unit - convert all units owned by you on a square to 4x that many resources. Resources are explained below.
  • Build Wall - convert all units owned by you on a square to a wall with 2x that much health. Walls are explained below.

Resolution

All players make moves at the same time. At the end of the day (midnight EST), resolution happens. The following stages occur:

  1. Any square on which there is a wall and a number of units, the wall's health will be reduced by a number equal to the number of units on the square. If the wall's health is reduced to zero, the wall is destroyed.
  2. For any square on which there are units belonging to more than one player, a battle will take place. Using a Risk-like methodology, units will be removed until only one player's units are left, and that player will then control that square. The player with more units has a higher likelihood of winning.
  3. Any square on which there is only a single player's unit becomes owned by that player.
  4. Any square with a resource amount that is owned by a player will generate one unit for that player and reduce the number of resources on that square by one.

Resources

Battles reduce the number of units you control, and resources are the way you get more units. As previously discussed, controlling a square that has resources will gain you one unit per turn, but this also drains the resources on that square. Settling units replenishes the resources on a square. Since you gain 4 resources for every one unit committed, settling is like an investment: you set aside units now in order to gain 4x that many over the course of several turns.

Walls

Unlike some games, walls are not owned by any player. When you build a wall, you forfeit that square. However, because the builder chooses where a wall is placed, you can slow the spread of an enemy with them. Anyone can attack a wall at any time. Settlements can not be created on a walled square, and walls can not be placed on a settlement.
